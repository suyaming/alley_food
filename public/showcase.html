v2 · 朱印

Vermilion stamp. Strongest recall, scales down to 24px and stays legible. Used as the default site mark.

Six variants

v1

门洞 Door Arch

A literal alley entrance reduced to two strokes. Most abstract, most quiet. Reads well at any size, but the brand name lives only in the wordmark beside it.

RECOMMENDED

v2

朱印 Vermilion Stamp

The Chinese hanko reduced to one block of vermilion + two characters. The corner notch hints at hand-pressing. Strongest identity — works as packaging seal, signboard, and small favicon.

v3

夜灯 Alley Lantern

One-point perspective walls + a single lantern. Tells the story in three primitives. The most romantic mark, but the least legible at small scale.

v4

单字 One Character

The kou (mouth) radical, alone. The vermilion dot reads as punctuation, a hanging lantern, or a small flame — let the viewer decide.

v5

杂志排印 Editorial Stack

A self-contained masthead. Carries the entire brand in one composition — eyebrow, mark, rule, footer. Use on covers and opening spreads, not in the header chrome.

v6

点阵 Dotted Hutong

The kou character drawn out as a 7×7 dot ring. Negative space does the work. Reads modern, ages well, and animates nicely if you ever need motion.
